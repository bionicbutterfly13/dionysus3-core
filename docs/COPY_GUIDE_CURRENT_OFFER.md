## 3. Voice & Formatting Rules

- **No m-dashes.** Use commas, periods, colons, or line breaks. Breath breaks via spacing or colons, not em- or en-dashes.
- **Concrete, sensory, vivid.** Every experiential benefit = a **cinematic snapshot**: real moment, sights/sounds/context. No floaty or abstract summaries.
- **Avatar-grounded.** Lock copy to what **analytical empath + ADHD** actually experience (e.g. Zoom call, pitch deck, spiral, abandon, restart).
- **Copy-paste-ready.** Keep objection/claim/proof/benefit blocks clean. No filler between meat.

## 4. What We Avoid (Never Frame As)

- Inconsistency or avoidance as character flaws.
- “Self-sabotage” without mechanism.
- Willpower, mindset shifts, or “just believe in yourself” as the fix.
- Needing to relive trauma, calm down first, or meditate 20 minutes before clarity.

## 5. Lead Narrative Flow (Mechanism-Based Campaign)

1. **Open with agitation.** *“If you’ve ever spent hours spiraling in mental replay and then abandoned something that mattered… it’s not because you’re inconsistent.”*
2. **Expose the invisible loop.** Replay → Abandon → Restart. Frame as neurological prediction error, not personal flaw.
3. **Create the gap.** *“Most solutions assume you can pull yourself back mid-collapse. When the system predicts unrecoverability, that’s biologically impossible.”*
4. **Introduce the mechanism.** Neuroadaptive Scaffolding: externalizes signal, clarity, and motion.
5. **Prove it.** Loops shorten from hours to minutes; no emotional excavation; clients resume *inside* the same context.
6. **Make the mechanism the star.** *“This isn’t about changing who you are. It’s about restoring your ability to re-enter the task, no matter your state.”*
