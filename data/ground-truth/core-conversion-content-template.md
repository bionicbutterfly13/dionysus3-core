### Client Story #1 âœ“ COMPLETE
**Name:** Dr. Danielle
**Full Story:** See `data/ground-truth/client-story-dr-danielle.md`

**Condensed Version (for script):**

**Before:**
Rural physician. Nice home, well-behaved kids, loving husband at a Fortune 100. Community leader. Partnership track. From the outside looking in, she had it all.

But she'd lie awake wondering, "Is this it?" Each promotion felt like more of the same: more responsibility, more emptiness. Patients started complaining she seemed distant. She went from being the clinic's rising star to someone who just didn't want to be bothered.

**Belief Origin:**
Her parents worked hard and sacrificed for her. They taught her: work hard, get more for your family. She learned to equate success with suffering. The harder you work, the more you deserve. Rest is for people who haven't earned it yet.

**Discovery:**
We uncovered her hidden belief: "The more successful you get, the more miserable you're supposed to be." She'd been punishing herself with service, absorbing everyone's burdens, never letting herself rest because she didn't feel she'd earned it.

**The Moment:**
She was advising her husband to go home early. Mid-sentence, she realized: she needed to take her own advice. Then a patient whose husband had just passed said, "I wish I had listened more." Dr. Danielle started wondering: how much was she actually hearing her husband? They talked every day. But was anyone really there?

**After:**
Now she and her husband do co-working sessions on Tuesdays. She catches herself when she slips into autopilot. She specialized her talks: physician well-being, maternal stress. She's living her science. She's not chasing the next level. She's present where she is.

**Hell Island (avoided):**
Five more years of detachment. Husband mirroring her workaholism. Kids raising themselves. Deep regret when the oldest left for college. Son always working at school because that's what was modeled.

**Heaven Island (now):**
Consciously engineering her days. Advocating for what she prefers instead of assuming suffering into situations. Living her science. It means all the world to her.
