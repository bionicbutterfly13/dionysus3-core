### **PART 3: THE TEACHING PHASE**

*(This is the core content delivery. It teaches the "what" but not the "how," establishing expertise and providing immense value. It focuses on the 3 IAS phases.)*

**\[SLIDE 18: Title of section: "The 3-Phase Framework to Reclaim Your Power"\]**

Okay, so there are three phases to this journey. The same three phases we guide every single one of our clients through. Think of it like this: Reveal, Ignite, and Align.

**\[SLIDE 19: Phase 1: REVEAL \- "Expose the hidden sabotage loop." with a magnifying glass icon\]**

**Phase One is REVEAL.** The promise here is to expose the hidden sabotage loop that quietly drives every setback.

You can't change a pattern you can't see. So the first step is always to move from being limited by unconscious patterns to having the clarity to see your own internal system.

For example, in our first lesson, *Breakthrough Mapping*, we guide you to do three things:

1. **Choose** the specific, frustrating pattern you want to address. Maybe it's procrastination on a big project.  
2. **Pinpoint** the underlying belief driving it. You ask, "What would have to be true for me to keep choosing this pattern?" For a client of ours, a professor, her belief was, "My worth as a scholar depends on never being rejected."  
3. **Notice** the self-talk that emerges from that belief. Her self-talk was, "This isn't good enough yet. I need more research." This created the self-sabotage of avoiding submission.

In this phase, you become the detective of your own mind. You're not judging, you're just mapping the system. This is how you go from being unknowingly held back to consciously aware of exactly what needs to change.

**\[SLIDE 20: Phase 2: IGNITE \- "Recode that loop fast—spark the new identity." with a spark icon\]**

**Phase Two is IGNITE.** Once you see the old pattern, you need to recode it and spark the new identity in real time.

This is where you release outdated beliefs and choose truth over perfection. This isn't about positive thinking. It's about building real conviction.

For example, in our lesson called the *Conviction Gauntlet*, we guide you to put your old belief on trial.

1. You **review** the old belief—like the professor's "If I'm rejected, I'm not qualified."  
2. You **analyze** the "evidence." She realized the evidence was one harsh comment from a mentor years ago, versus a decade of successful work.  
3. You **develop and test** a new, empowering belief. Her new belief wasn't "I'm a genius." It was something more grounded and believable: "My value grows when I learn from feedback, not when I'm flawless." She tested this by submitting a paper she knew wasn't "perfect," and the world didn't end. In fact, she got helpful feedback.

This phase is about moving from belief *disruption* to integrating new, empowering convictions that feel true at a gut level.

**\[SLIDE 21: Phase 3: ALIGN \- "Embed the identity so actions and results stay congruent." with an anchor icon\]**

**Phase Three is ALIGN.** This is where the transformation becomes second nature. The goal is to embed the new identity so your actions, habits, and results stay congruent automatically.

This is what prevents relapse into old patterns. It’s about making your new way of being your default state.

For instance, in our *Habit Harmonizer* lesson, you learn to anchor your new beliefs through daily practice.

1. You **internalize** the new belief through structured reflection, like a 2-minute daily check-in.  
2. You **apply** it in small, real-life scenarios. You practice taking up space in a meeting or setting a boundary with a colleague.  
3. You build a simple **Daily Belief Alignment Checklist** to create consistency, turning a fragile new thought into a fortified belief.

This is how success begins to flow from your authentic self, rather than from pure, exhausting effort.

**\[SLIDE 22: Recap of the 3 Phases: Reveal \-\> Ignite \-\> Align\]**

So that's the framework. Reveal the pattern, Ignite the change, and Align your actions. It's a system. A repeatable process for getting out of your own way.

Can you see how this is different from just reading another self-help book? You’re not just collecting ideas. You’re re-architecting your inner world.
